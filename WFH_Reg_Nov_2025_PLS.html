Phishing — How to recognise & respond
Short guide for all employees

What is phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where attackers impersonate trusted organisations or people to trick you into revealing sensitive information (passwords, personal data, or banking details) or to get you to click malicious links and attachments.

Common examples

  • Fake HR emails asking to "confirm your payroll" from a non‑company domain.
  • Urgent security alerts that request immediate password change via a link.
  • Emails with attachments (invoice.zip, hr_document.pdf.exe) that execute malware.

How to spot a phishing email

  • Check the sender's domain carefully — the text before the "@" can look correct but the domain after may be wrong (e.g. hr.prana4life@outlook.com vs hr@prana4life.com).
  • Look for poor grammar, spelling mistakes, or odd salutations.
  • Hover (don't click) links to see the actual URL — if it doesn't match the claimed organisation, do not click.
  • Unexpected attachments or asks for credentials are major red flags.

Real quick examples

Phishing subject: "Payroll verification required - Immediate action"
Why suspicious: Sender domain is not the company's domain; contains a short link that redirects to an external site.
Phishing subject: "Urgent: Update your password"
Why suspicious: A legitimate password reset would come from it@prana4life.com and include the employee's name or be performed via our internal portal.

If you suspect a phishing email — immediate steps

  • Do NOT click any links or open attachments.
  • Do NOT reply to the sender.
  • Take a screenshot of the email (do not crop headers) and forward it to it@prana4life.com with the subject "Suspected Phishing".
  • If you clicked a link or entered credentials, change your password immediately and inform IT by phone/Teams as well.
